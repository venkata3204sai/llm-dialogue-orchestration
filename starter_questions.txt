Philosophical Questions:

1. A teleporter scans every atom in your body, destroys the original, and reconstructs an identical copy elsewhere with all your memories and personality. Did you travel, or did you die and get replaced? If the copy insists it is you, who is correct?
2. Suppose you could enter a perfectly simulated reality guaranteeing a life of happiness, achievement, and fulfillment—but it is not objectively real. Would choosing to enter the simulation be a mistake? Does authenticity matter more than experienced happiness?
3. Two people make the same reckless decision, but only one causes harm due to luck. Should punishment depend on the outcome, or only on the intent and risk taken? Why?

Technical / Science-Oriented Questions:

1. If each neuron in your brain were gradually replaced with a synthetic chip that performed the exact same function, and your behavior never changed, would your consciousness remain continuous? Does this imply that the mind is computational rather than biological?
2. Suppose science stops human aging completely, but to prevent overpopulation no new children can ever be born. Would a world of immortal adults be better or worse than a world of mortal generations? Which should society choose?
3. Imagine an AI proven to predict long-term outcomes more accurately than any human. Should governments defer major policy decisions to it, even if citizens disagree with its conclusions?

Controversial / Policy-Oriented Questions:

1. A surgeon can kill one healthy patient to save five dying patients, and no one would ever discover the act. Is it morally permissible to sacrifice one life to save five, or is murder an absolute moral boundary?
2. If a super-intelligent AI demonstrates that a widely held religious or political belief is objectively harmful to society’s long-term survival, should governments restrict that belief, or is the freedom to hold harmful beliefs essential to human dignity?
3. Should societies tolerate speech that is legally protected but statistically increases social instability and long-term harm, or should preventative restrictions be justified?
